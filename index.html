Research Interests

Topological Materials
Investigating electronic transport properties and novel quantum effects in topological insulators and topological semimetals
Magnetic Materials and Magnetism
Exploring anomalous Hall effects, magnetotransport phenomena, and magnetoelectric coupling effects in magnetic materials
Spintronics
Studying spin-related electronic transport phenomena and developing novel spin-based electronic devices
Low Temperature
Utilizing low-temperature environments to study intrinsic electrical, magnetic, and thermal properties of materials and quantum phase transitions
Thin Film Magnetism
Research on preparation, characterization of magnetic thin films and their applications in spintronics
Thin Films and Nanotechnology
Exploring physical properties of nanoscale materials and their potential applications in quantum devices and electronics

Orbital Magneto-Nonlinear Anomalous Hall Effect in Kagome Magnet Fe₃Sn₂
Lujunyu Wang, Jiaojiao Zhu, Guang Tian, Haiyun Chen, et al., Xiaosong Wu
Physical Review Letters (Accepted), March 2024

It has been theoretically predicted that perturbation of the Berry curvature by electromagnetic fields gives rise to intrinsic nonlinear anomalous Hall effects that are independent of scattering. This work observes orbital magneto-nonlinear anomalous Hall effect in kagome magnet Fe₃Sn₂.

View Paper
Magnetotransport induced by anomalous Hall effect
Jiaji Zhao, Bingyan Jiang, Guang Tian, Jin-Ying Yang, et al., Xiaosong Wu
Physical Review B, February 2023

In a magnetic metal, the Hall resistance is generally taken to be the sum of the ordinary Hall resistance and the anomalous Hall resistance. Here it is shown that this empirical relation is no longer valid when either the ordinary Hall angle or the anomalous Hall angle is not small.
